Zoo Animals

this page will give valuable information on animals that are kept in the zoo.

Bears

Bears can be as small as four feet long and about 60 pounds (the sun bear) to as big as eight feet long and more than a thousand pounds (the polar bear). They're found throughout North America, South America, Europe, and Asia. They have nonretractable claws, short tails, and excellent sense of smell.

Giraffes

Giraffes are the tallest land animals in the world, with males (bulls) standing up to 18 feet (5.5 meters) tall at the shoulder and horns. They have long necks, which can extend up to 2 meters, and long legs, short bodies, and tufted tails. Giraffes are covered in pale buff coats with reddish brown spots, and their skin is heavily pigmented.

Lions

Lions (Panthera leo) are large cats native to Africa and India with muscular, broad-chested bodies, short, rounded heads, round ears, and hairy tail tufts. They are the second largest member of the cat family, after tigers, and have sharp claws and strong teeth.

Alligators

Alligators have a long, rounded snout that has upward facing nostrils at the end; this allows breathing to occur while the rest of the body is underwater. The young have bright yellow stripes on the tail; adults have dark stripes on the tail. It's easy to distinguish an alligator from a crocodile by the teeth.
